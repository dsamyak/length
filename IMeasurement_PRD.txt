EXECUTIVE SUMMARYIMeasurement is a gamified, simulation-driven web experience built for Singapore Primary 1 students (Age 6–7) covering the mathematics concept of Length — specifically the comparison of objects as 'longer' or 'shorter'. The product is hosted as an interactive lesson on intelliasg.com/courses/grade-1-math under Module 5.1 of the Grade 1 Math curriculum.The experience is architected around the proven pedagogical approach demonstrated at numberbound.vercel.app — a combination of animated story-driven learning, interactive simulations, and gamified progressive practice. Students first engage with concept introduction through cartoon characters and real-world visual metaphors, then move into simulation-based exploration, and finally test their understanding through a bank of 100+ randomly generated practice questions.The application aligns strictly with the Singapore Ministry of Education (MOE) Primary 1 Mathematics syllabus and Intellia SG's curriculum framework, ensuring academic rigour while maintaining age-appropriate engagement, inclusive design, and accessible audio-visual interactivity.2. CURRICULUM & LEARNING OBJECTIVES (Singapore MOE Aligned)4.1 MOE Syllabus ReferenceThis lesson maps to Singapore MOE Primary 1 Mathematics Syllabus, Strand: Measurement and Data. The specific competency addressed is: 'Comparing the lengths of two or more objects using terms such as longer, shorter, tallest, shortest.'4.2 Learning ObjectivesStudents can identify which of two objects is longer and which is shorterStudents can use the vocabulary: longer, shorter, longest, shortest, same lengthStudents can compare lengths by direct comparison (placing objects side by side)Students can identify length relationships when objects are not aligned at the same starting pointStudents can order 3 or more objects from shortest to longest and vice versaStudents can apply length comparison to real-world familiar objects (pencils, ribbons, snakes, towers)4.3 Conceptual Progression (CPA Approach)The lesson follows Singapore's Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) instructional framework:PhaseActivity TypeImplementationCONCRETEPhysical understandingSimulation: Drag & compare virtual rulers/objectsPICTORIALVisual comparisonSide-by-side image comparison with labelled arrowsABSTRACTSymbolic understandingWord-based questions using 'longer', 'shorter'5.
